UMass Amherst Bachelor's Degree w/ Individual Concentration:

Community Economics

3.9 GPA

Community Economics is an interdisciplinary concentration centered around the intersection of the related topics of economics, urban planning, and political thought.

It is fundamentally a program designed to inform a deeper understanding of the modern issues affecting communities across the globe.

At the core of the curriculum is a focus on grassroots political activism and community-based solutions to scalable social, economic, and environmental problems that include:

  • climate change adaptation and urban resiliency

  • environmental conservation & ecosystem preservation

  • functional food systems, permaculture, urban agriculture projects

  • small business development and community placemaking

  • and, in general, building fairer and more equitable economies.
