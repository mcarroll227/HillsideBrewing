Matthew Carroll

April 6th, 2022

 

Powa by the Tune-Yards

 

It’s a great song for what felt like the first really amazing spring day of the year. I was sitting with my partner by the window when I finally started work on the draft of my transcription, listening to the song for the hundredth time and she, totally unprompted, just started spouting colors and feelings. It really felt like the perfect song for such an uncharacteristically warm, windy April day. Not to mention our flower bed had just started blooming. It was a really happy song for a happy afternoon.

 

I knew I wanted to work with graphics somehow, but while I was looking for pictures of flowers at first to copy/paste into my template I came across this amazing National Geographic video of all of these amazing flowers blooming into all these insane different colors. Even without messing with the video, it already kind of fit. The flowers were perfect for the sweet, gentle, flowing sound of the ukulele.

 

So, I decided to use the colors of the flowers to represent the timbre & texture of each chord and the rate / direction of their bloom to follow the melody. As the notes got higher in pitch, the flowers would bloom and, when they dipped a little, I split the video and reversed it as the notes descended further down the scale, so that the flowers would begin to close. Or, if the pitch got really low, they’d close completely.

 

I thought the slow and steady vertical bloom of the first (blue) flower worked well with the fingerpicking, while the fast/loud chords worked well with the pink and orange buds that sort of exploded into those amazing flowers. For that final distorted chord in the verse, and the sort of echo that follows, I wanted the flowers to close. I’m also glad I was able to figure out how to do the side-by-side configuration for that crazy spaceship-looking flower for the crazy sustained reverb around 0:40 (which was admittedly the whole reason I chose the song).

 

But for the power chords after the drop (on the guitar), I knew the flowers wouldn’t work. Cows seemed like the obvious solution. Big, powerful chords! Sturdy, steady, and awesome. Each of the three cows represents the three chords that made their way down the scale in pitch, interrupted by that final weird, sharp chord that I felt like could only be correctly represented by that short clip of the chicken. Was that a string being bent, or an effect? Maybe a slide? I don’t know. Chicken.

 

The chord progression, after the drop, just went like this:

COW, Cow, cow – HEN – cow, Cow, COW

COW, Cow, cow – HEN – cow, Cow, COW

 

For the rap-tap-tapping on the edge of the drum around the middle of the song, I also included a subtle overlay of a bunch of little Java crabs going absolutely crazy. They’re hard to see, but I felt like the clickyness or, like, snappyness of the percussion part justified a subtle, but interesting little overlay. I thought the original David Attenborough video with like 100,000 tiny little crabs was perfect for it.

 

Last, the cloud overlay, with the clouds floating up, was supposed to represent the singer’s vocals, which were super breathy, or, airy? Her voice is so light and lovely – it just made me think of clouds.
