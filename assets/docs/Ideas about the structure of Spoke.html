Ideas about the structure of Spoke

 

Assumptions

 

  • Each square is a measure, represents four beats.
  • Each column is meant to be played simultaneously
  • Each row is it’s own instrument
  • Each shape represents a CHORD
  • Size of shape represents VOLUME
  • Position of shape within the square represents PITCH

 

waves:

  • a series up up/down chords descending as the waves become lower in their square
  • Ex;  f#, g# (2nd String; 7,9), then down the fretboard (vert? horiz? idk)

 

blocks:

  • playing in a square
  • like a square dance
  • spirals moving counterclockwise? play counterclockwise?

 

wind:

 

 

 

INSTRUCTIONS:

 

Waves - Unit #1

  • three high, half note slides
  • three lower half note slides
  • a tiny blip slide (fast)
  • four lower half note slides
  • four and a half (lower)
  • another four and a half (lower
  • 1/16th notes

 

Blocks - Unit #2

  • four eighth notes, arranged in a square (real low on major scale, E and A strings)
  • same, higher (D and G strings)
  • low, on E and A strings (on blues scale, because bigger and notes are further apart
  • medium, but not as high as before. All on the D string (only two measures)

 

Blocks - Unit #3

  • low, on E and A strings (repeat jazz block)
  • low on major scale, E and A strings repeat first block)
  • repeat last block from Unit #2 on D string (with four notes)

 

Swirl - Unit #4

 

  • Amaj chord slide up and down between Open and 7th fret
  • eight times fast

 

Wind - Unit #5

 

  • Slide C barred DOWN, UP, AND DOWN AGAIN
  • Repeat three times, higher each time

 

Stairs - Unit #6

 

  • eighth notes on the maj scale (starting low)

 

Wheat - Unit #7

 

  • Bar 3rd fret and fingerpick from 6th to 1st string
  • repeat with 5th, 7th, and 9th

 

Desert - Unit #8

 

  • Same as Unit #1 (waves), instead using 1/4notes
  • Alternate high/low/high and low/high/low
  • Each gets three (six alternations)

 

Circles - Unit #9

 

  • SLIDE just D and G strings between 2nd and 4th frets
  • (almost an Aminor)
  • Each gets three slides
  • Repeat with G/B and E/A

 

Circles - Unit #10

 

  • SLIDE just D and G strings between 4nd and 6th frets (higher than first in Unit #8)
  • Slide E and A, again higher than before
  • Slide B/E
  • Again, each gets three slides

 

Wind - Unit #10

 

  • Slide F# barred DOWN, UP, AND DOWN AGAIN
  • Repeat three times
  • Higher each time
  • (more dramatic reversals (up/down) than Unit #4

 

Ripples - Unit #11

 

  • Slide Eminor 
  • DOWN, UP, DOWN, UP, AND DOWN (then up a little)

 

Grass - Unit #12

 

  • 12 groups of three random consecutive notes (ie C, C#, D)
  • DESCENDING
  • FAST

 

Grass - Unit #12

 

  • Kinda like Unit 12, but more controlled
  • D string, B string, A string

 

Bubbles - Unit #13

 

  • Slide power chords a half note up and down
  • 2 rounds of 2
  • A/D + G/B

 

Bubbles - Unit #13

 

  • Slide power chords
  • 2 rounds of 2
  • A/D + E/A

 

Bubbles - Unit #14

  • Slide Power chords
  • 2 rounds of 2
  • B/e + G/B

 

Wheatgrass Blowing in the wind - Unit #15

 

  • Slide G frantically up the fret board
  • eight half steps

 

Wheatgrass Blowing in the wind - Unit #16

 

  • Slide F frantically up the fretboard
